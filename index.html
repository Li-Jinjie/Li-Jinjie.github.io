Research

Learning-Based MPC for Close Formation Tracking of Quadrotors
 Master’s Thesis, Ongoing

The downwash effect caused by other agents is a unique problem for aerial robotics and is hard to model. How could aerial robots observe the downwash effect and integrate it into the state-of-the-art trajectory tracking framework?

Abstract The thesis plans to study the close formation tracking problem of multiple quadrotors considering aerodynamic disturbance compensation, which is able to expand the application scenarios of UAV swarm technology. The thesis first models the aerodynamic disturbance through deep learning and then combines the disturbance model with a nominal quadrotor model. Next, the new model is integrated with Model Predictive Control (MPC) framework to design a trajectory tracking algorithm for UAVs. Finally, the proposed algorithm is verified in both simulation environments and physical platforms.

Indoor Localization for Quadrotors using Invisible Projected Tags
 Jinjie Li, Liang Han, Zhang Ren
International Conference on Robotics and Automation (ICRA), 2022
[oral] [arXiv] [video]

This work proposes a real-time centimeter-level indoor localization method based on invisible projected tags (IPT).

Abstract Augmented reality (AR) technology has been introduced into the robotics field to narrow the visual gap between indoor and outdoor environments. However, without signals from satellite navigation systems, flight experiments in these indoor AR scenarios need other accurate localization approaches. This work proposes a real-time centimeter-level indoor localization method based on psycho-visually invisible projected tags (IPT), requiring a projector as the sender and quadrotors with high-speed cameras as the receiver. The method includes a modulation process for the sender, as well as demodulation and pose estimation steps for the receiver, where screen-camera communication technology is applied to hide fiducial tags using human vision property. Experiments have demonstrated that IPT can achieve accuracy within ten centimeters and a speed of about ten FPS. Compared with other localization methods for AR robotics platforms, IPT is affordable by using only a projector and high-speed cameras as hardware consumption and convenient by omitting a coordinate alignment step. To the authors' best knowledge, this is the first time screen-camera communication is utilized for AR robot localization.

Formation Control for Quadrotors with RL and Visual Fiducial Localization
 Outstanding Bachelor’s Thesis, 2020
[thesis_zh] [code]

The thesis contains three parts. (1) The fiducial tags on the ground are captured for visual positioning, and the results are combined with IMU to improve reliability and accuracy; (2) Dyna-Q reinforcement learning algorithm is implemented to train a multi-quadrotor system for formation control; (3) The algorithms are verified in a ROS/Gazebo-based simulation platform. The thesis is ranked No.1 in my major.

Abstract In recent years, the vigorous development of computer vision and artificial intelligence has created opportunities for more intelligent drones. This thesis implements the fiducial-tag positioning method and the Dyna-Q reinforcement learning algorithm in a multi-UAV formation control system. This combination improves the autonomy and anti-interference of UAVs in complex environments.
The thesis contains three parts. For the first part, the basic principles of visual positioning are studied. Then, the basic knowledge of computer vision is applied to achieve camera calibration, tags detection, decoding, and pose solving to obtain pose data. Next, the pose data is integrated with an inertial measurement unit (IMU) to provide continuous and accurate positions in the sparse-tag scenario. For the second part, the formation problem is decomposed into a target tracking task and a collision avoidance task, which are designed as Markov Decision Processes (MDPs), respectively. Then the Dyna-Q algorithm is utilized for training these tasks. Finally, the positioning and formation control task of five drones are completed in a ROS/Gazebo-based simulation platform, achieving less than 3cm formation error.

Development of a 3D Simulator for Large-Scale Heterogeneous Swarm Robots
 Supported by the Science and Technology Innovation 2030-Key Project of “New Generation Artificial Intelligence”, Student Software Architect, 2020.09 - Now
[screen recording]

Develop a large-scale swarm simulation platform. The number of supported nodes in the simulation must be ‚â• 1000; at least four types of agents should be supported, including multi-rotor, tilt-rotor, fixed-wing, and autonomous vehicles.

Tech Details The simulation platform is divided into three sides: display side, physics side, and algorithm side. The three sides run on different computers to facilitate functional decoupling and remote development. In actual implementation, the algorithm side sends control commands to the physics side, which performs dynamic simulation. The physics side then sends states of all nodes at the new moment back to the algorithm side to complete a control loop. At the same time, the physics side continuously sends the states to the display side for display.

Design of Heavy Load and High Maneuverability Aircrafts
 China Aeromodelling Design Challenge (CADC), Leader of the Composite Material Team & Pilot, 2017.07 - 2018.10
[BMFA News Magazine report] [tech details & video] [pilot training & video]

Designed and produced the composite part of a 5m-wingspan aircraft with the maximum load of 24kg, the maximum take-off weight of 27.5kg, and the nominal airspeed of 15m/s. Applied the carbon-PMI-carbon sandwich structure to make the 130g-weighted single wing beam, outer twined with the Kevlar line; utilized carbon and glass fiber reinforced polymer (CGFRP) to make D-box structure, increasing the torsional rigidity to 261.07%. Won the top three places in the 2018 CADC (Time-limited Airdrop Project), the best record in history. Served as the pilot of solar aircrafts as well.

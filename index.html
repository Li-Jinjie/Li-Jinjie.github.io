Research

Indoor Positioning for Quadrotors using Invisible Projected Tags
 Jinjie Li, Liang Han, Zhang Ren
International Conference on Robotics and Automation (ICRA), 2022
[arXiv] [video] [code] [bib]

This work proposes a real-time centimeter-level indoor positioning method based on invisible projected tags (IPT).

Abstract Augmented reality (AR) technology has been introduced into the robotics field to narrow the visual gap between indoor and outdoor environments. However, without signals from satellite navigation systems, flight experiments in these indoor AR scenarios need other accurate positioning approaches. This work proposes a real-time centimeter-level indoor positioning method based on invisible projected tags (IPT), requiring a projector as the sender and quadrotors with high-speed cameras as the receiver. The method includes the modulation process for the sender, as well as the demodulation and pose estimation steps for the receiver, where screen-camera communication technology is applied to hide fiducial tags using human vision property. Experiments have demonstrated that IPT can achieve accuracy within four centimeters and a speed of about ten FPS, satisfying the demand for indoor flights. Compared with other positioning methods for AR robotics platforms, IPT is convenient by omitting a coordinate alignment step and affordable by using only a projector and high-speed cameras as hardware consumption.
